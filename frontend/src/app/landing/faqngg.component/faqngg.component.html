A bulk order generally refers to purchasing a large quantity of items in a single transaction. It usually means buying products in wholesale amounts rather than individual pieces. Businesses often set a minimum threshold (like 50, 100, or more units) to qualify as bulk. Such orders often come with discounts, special pricing, or customized packaging. They are common in retail, manufacturing, and corporate supply purchases.

The most suppliers allow customization for bulk orders. You can often choose product colors, sizes, or packaging styles. Some businesses even offer branding options like logos or labels. Customization may require a minimum order quantity or extra cost. It is best to confirm details with the supplier before placing the order.

The minimum order quantity (MOQ) for wholesale rates varies by supplier and product type. It refers to the smallest number of units a supplier is willing to sell at one time. For common items, MOQs can be as low as 10 or 50 units, while custom products may require 500 or 1,000 units. Suppliers set MOQs to balance production efficiency, pricing, and inventory control. Always check with the specific supplier, as requirements differ widely

The most suppliers provide a detailed price list for bulk orders. It usually includes unit prices, discounts, and tiered rates based on quantity. Some lists also mention customization charges or shipping costs. Price lists help buyers compare options and plan budgets effectively. Always request the latest version, as prices may change over time.
